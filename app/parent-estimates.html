Lifecycle of a Failed Project

How timelines and budgets grow

IT Projects rarely fail all at once. Instead, they tend to snowball, growing larger and more hopeless as time goes on. Along the way, the definition of "success" is prone to mutation, as deadlines get pushed back and budgets increase. That's how a project can launch "ahead of schedule" even if it's more than three years late.

To illustrate this evolution toward catastrophe, we've reconstucted the budget and deadline history of a handful of of the most egregious IT project debacles of the last decade.
